1. The Energy Mix Map (Market Share Analysis)
The Insight: There is a stark geographic divide in the energy transition. North America, parts of South America (Brazil), and Northern Europe are leading (Green), while Asia and parts of Africa remain heavily reliant on fossil fuels (Red).
Business Justification: This map acts as a Risk and Opportunity Heatmap. For an energy investor, the "Red" zones represent high-risk areas for future carbon taxes but massive opportunities for infrastructure development. For a policy maker, it identifies which regions need the most aggressive subsidy interventions to meet global climate targets.

2. Fossil Fuel Phase-Out Leaderboard
The Insight: Brazil is significantly outpacing every other nation in reducing absolute fossil fuel generation, while others like Mexico and Japan show moderate but steady declines.
Business Justification: It proves that aggressive phase-out is possible at scale. Brazil serves as a proof for other developing nations. From a business perspective, the companies operating in the top-ranked countries are likely benefiting from mature green-energy regulatory frameworks, making them safer long-term bets.

3. Growth vs. Efficiency Scatter Plot
The Insight: Solar Energy is the clear "High-Growth, Low-Efficiency" outlier , while Bioenergy and others are "Low-Growth, High-Efficiency" . Wind energy sits in the middle as a maturing, moderate-performance asset.
Business Justification:
Solar is where the capital is flowing (High Growth), but it requires the most technological innovation to improve its efficiency ratio.
Bioenergy provides the grid stability (High Efficiency) that renewables often lack, making it a critical  partner for volatile sources like solar.
Wind appears to be entering a phase where growth is cooling, potentially due to market saturation or high interest rates for offshore projects.
